Interview Prep Guide

How to find, evaluate and hire winning sales reps — every time

What to Look For

These 5 traits predict success. Score each candidate against them.

🏆 Coachability

Can they take feedback without getting defensive? This is #1. You can teach skills, you can't teach attitude.

🔥 Hunger

Do they NEED to make money? Financial motivation drives results. Someone comfortable won't push hard enough.

📈 Sales Experience

Have they sold B2B before? Real estate, insurance, SaaS, or any professional services is ideal.

📱 Tech Comfort

Can they use basic software? They don't need to be technical, just comfortable using tools and apps.

💬 Communication

Are they clear, confident, articulate? If they can't sell themselves in the interview, they can't sell to agents.

🔴 Red Flags

Makes excuses, blames others for past failures, vague answers, no questions for you, asks about salary guarantee first.

Interview Questions

Ask all 7. Listen for specifics, not generalities. Good reps give real examples.

1"Walk me through your sales process from first contact to close."
✓ GOOD ANSWER
Describes specific steps. Mentions prospecting, qualifying, demo, follow-up, handling objections, close. Shows they have a system.
✗ RED FLAG
Vague. Says "I just talk to people." No process. Can't explain what they actually do. No structure = no results.
2"Tell me about your biggest sale. How did you close it?"
✓ GOOD ANSWER
Gives a specific deal size, explains what obstacle they overcame, takes personal credit. Can replay the close moment-by-moment.
✗ RED FLAG
Can't remember details. Downplays role ("the team helped"). No specific number. If they can't recall their best win, it wasn't meaningful.
3"What do you do when someone says no?"
✓ GOOD ANSWER
"I find out why, address the real objection, and follow up later." They see no as a starting point, not an ending.
✗ RED FLAG
"I move on." or "I respect their decision." Sales is about persistence. Someone who gives up on no won't close anyone.
4"What do you know about LuxeLeadPro?"
✓ GOOD ANSWER
Visited the site. Understands it's AI lead scoring for luxury agents. Can explain the value prop in their own words. Shows initiative.
✗ RED FLAG
Nothing prepared. "I just saw the job posting." Didn't spend 5 minutes researching. If they won't prepare for an interview, they won't prepare for calls.
5"How do you handle rejection?"
✓ GOOD ANSWER
Treats it as part of the game. "Every no gets me closer to a yes." Has specific mental strategies. Stays motivated and doesn't take it personally.
✗ RED FLAG
Takes it personally. Gets demotivated. Needs to vent. In sales you hear no 80% of the time — this person will burn out in week 2.
6"What's your income goal this year?"
✓ GOOD ANSWER
Specific number. Hungry. "I want to make $80k this year." Driven. Has a reason (family, debt, goal). The more specific, the better.
✗ RED FLAG
"I don't know" or something low and comfortable. No clear target = no drive. You want someone who needs to close deals.
7"Why do you want to work on commission?"
✓ GOOD ANSWER
"I love that my earnings are in my hands." Confident in their ability to close. Sees unlimited upside as a feature, not a risk.
✗ RED FLAG
"I need some guaranteed base." Wrong fit for this role. Commission-only requires full confidence. Hesitation here is disqualifying.

🔴 Instant Disqualifiers

See any of these? End the interview politely and move on.

!
Late with no explanation or apology — If they can't show up on time for an interview, they won't show up for customers.
!
Talks badly about previous employers — They'll talk badly about you too. Unprofessional and a major warning sign.
!
Can't give specific examples — Everything is vague and general. Real salespeople have real stories. No stories = no experience.
!
Doesn't ask a single question — Curious people ask questions. Sales requires curiosity. Someone who doesn't ask is passive, not a hunter.
!
First question is about guaranteed salary — This is a commission role. If their first concern is guarantees, they're the wrong fit entirely.
!
Seems desperate for any job — Desperation = poor judgment = bad representation of your brand. You want someone who CHOOSES this, not someone who has no options.

🎤 Your Pitch to Them

Use this script when explaining the role. Word-for-word.

"We're building the sales team for LuxeLeadPro — AI-powered lead scoring for luxury real estate agents. Your job is simple: reach out to agents, book demos, close subscriptions. Commission is $50 to $200 per month per customer you close — and it stacks. Every customer you close keeps paying you every single month. 10 customers means $500 to $2,000 per month in recurring income. There's no cap. We give you every tool, all the training, and all the materials. You just need to show up and sell."

💡 Tip: Let that land. Don't rush past it. The best reps will start doing the math in their head and get excited.

After the Interview

Move fast. Great candidates have options. Same-day follow-up wins.

Same Day
Send a thank-you email to everyone you interviewed. Even passes. It's professional and they might refer someone better.
If YES — 24 hrs
Send the contractor agreement immediately. Fill in their name, start date, commission rates ($50/$75 base/outreach). Use DocuSign free tier.
If NO — 48 hrs
Send a polite rejection: "We've decided to move forward with another candidate. We appreciate your time and will keep you in mind for future opportunities."
Day 1 Onboarding
Send: EMPLOYEE_TRAINING.html (12-slide deck) + EMPLOYEE_ONBOARDING_WELCOME.md + a personal welcome message from Rob.
Day 2-4
30-minute video call: walk through the product live, practice the pitch deck together, answer questions, give them their first target list.
Week 1
Set target: 30 prospecting outreach emails + 3 demos booked by end of week. Check in daily at first. Momentum matters.
